Overview

Purpose

[Hockey has been booming in Nevada ever since the NHL awarded an NHL franchise to Las Vegas. In 2017 there were a total of 4 ice skating rinks with public access. Since then, there are now 9 rinks with additional facilities in development. The increase in hockey interest has led to increased demand for hockey instruction. The Black Hole Hockey Academy offers camps, clinics, and private instruction to beginning, intermediate and advanced level hockey players. Our state-of-the-art facility offers an array training programs including skating, stick handling, shooting, weight training, and tactical strategy. Our website will allow southern Nevada residents to sign up for the many offerings we provide.]

Audience

[The growing Las Vegas hockey community is my target. I want to assist in spurring the growth of hockey. As the sport is becoming more popular, parents and new players will want to find a place that will help them develop to become their best. ]

Content

Home page

The home page is going to lay out the options. I want it to look clear and concise. I want the main images on the page to be large and attention grabbing. The main page should be easy to navigate and lead users directly to where they want to go. Minimal in design and practical in its application. Whether a visitor is wishing to find information on clinics, or information private sessions, they’ll know where to find it. I also want to have the contact information and address easily displayed on the page. When visiting a website, I think visitors should be able to see the contact information without much effort. I want to have the address, phone number and email address listed in the either the head or the footer. For photos, I want to include at least 1 photo of kids playing hockey or skating and another photo of teenagers displaying more advanced mechanics or level of play. The colors of the page are mean to incorporate the natural colors of hockey. White = Ice, Black = Puck, Grey/Silver = Sticks/skates, red = goal posts. The font is meant to be sporty and sharp.

Clinic Page

Beginning Bambis: This clinic in tailored for newcomers to the sport. The class will focus primarily on skating, with some instruction on basic gameplay and hockey strategy. Participating will include a complimentary jersey and t-shirt. Full gear, helmets, and sticks will not be provided. Skates are available in the rental shop. (Included with this description will be a photo of children in a clinic) Dates: Monday - Friday. Times: 4:00-5:15 Rates: $20 Intermediate Mites: After passing a skating proficiency exam, our Bambis can move up to the mites clinic This clinic is catered towards beginners who making the jump to more competitive level. Skating instruction is more focused on power skating, and edge work. Stickhandling, shooting and passing is another emphasis. (Photo included will show older kids, 10-13 years old in a drill setting.) Dates: Monday – Friday. Times: 5:30-6:45 rates: $20 Advanced Astronauts: This special invite only clinic is meant to develop players to a college level skill or higher. These clinics include detailed skating technique for specific game situations. Drills will include 1 on 1 puck battles, 3 on 2 odd rushes, and forecheck strategy. Advanced clinic attendees are invited to try out for the traveling club teams (14 & under, 16 & under, 18 & under). Dates: Monday – Saturday, Times: 7:00-9:00. Rates: $20, free for members of club teams (Photo of high-level scrimmaging) Private lessons are available to skaters and goaltenders of all levels. Private lessons include: Skating, stick handling, shooting, passing, goaltending, weight training, and tactical training. Up 6 skaters can be included in one private session. Times: check availability, rates: $200/hour.

Coaches Page

Gordon Bombay, Head of Hockey Operations and Development: The former pro turned lawyer turned coach. Led the District 5 Mighty Ducks to a Minnesota Peewee championship, and Team USA to a Gold medal in the Goodwill Games. Peter Forsberg, Coach: Two time Stanley cup winner and Olympic Silver medalist for Sweden. Member of the hockey hall of fame. Known for his ability to keep puck possession and skate in high traffic area, he brings his years of experience to developing Las Vegas talent. Pokey Reddick, Goaltending coach: Former Las Vegas Thunder goaltender with a stint in the NHL with the Toronto Maple Leafs. Reddick has been coaching youth hockey in Southern Nevada for the last decade. Nate Schmidt, coach: Original Golden Misfit of the Vegas Golden Knights, former Washington Capitals and Winnipeg Jets defenseman. Ryan Reaves, coach: Former Pittsburgh Penguin, New York Ranger and Vegas Golden Knight. Reaves is a fan favorite in the league and known for his colorful personality and physical style of play.
